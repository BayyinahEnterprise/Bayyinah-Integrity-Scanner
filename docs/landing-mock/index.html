    How it Works

    Surface, substrate, and the gap between them.

    Every file has two layers. Bayyinah reads both, then reports where they disagree.

    ẒĀHIR

    Surface

    What the file presents. The rendered text, the visible cells, the page as a viewer or LLM ingests it.

    BĀṬIN

    Substrate

    What the file actually contains. Bytes, metadata, off-page streams, headers, embedded objects, comment payloads.

    BAYYINAH

    The Gap

    Reports the divergence by tier. Tier 1 verified concealment. Tier 2 structural anomaly. Tier 3 interpretive flag.

    Why this couldn't exist five years ago

    Four prerequisites. One convergence.

    Bayyinah is not a clever idea waiting on engineering. It is the first work that became possible after four independent threads landed.

    2022
    LLMs ingest documents at scale.
    Frontier models cross the threshold of reliable document understanding. The attack surface is created the same year it becomes useful.
    2023
    Adversarial prompt injection is demonstrated in the wild.
    The threat moves from theoretical to documented. Documents become a vector, not just a payload.
    2024
    Alignment faking is empirically observed.
    Greenblatt et al. demonstrate that frontier models cannot reliably self-verify their own input integrity. Any solution has to live outside the model.
    Greenblatt et al., 2024
    2025
    The Munafiq Protocol is formalized.
    A diagnostic framework for surface-substrate divergence, verified, structural, interpretive, becomes the basis for systematic file integrity verification.
    2026
    Bayyinah ships.
    The first document firewall built after all four prerequisites converged. v1.1.1 today, with a published miss list as the next milestone.
